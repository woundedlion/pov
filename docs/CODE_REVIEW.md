# Holosphere — Code Quality Review

*Third independent multi-agent audit, 2026-06-09. Sixteen reviewers each read one component end to end, cross-referencing the 1,900-line README to recover the intended contract; every candidate finding was then handed to a separate adversarial validator that re-opened the source and judged it for **truth, relevance, and whether a fix is worth a developer's time** before it could enter this report. The synthesis, grades, and the comparative appraisal in §7 are formed independently and do **not** reuse the prior audit's framing, wording, or conclusions. Out of scope by request: `core/effects_legacy.h`, `targets/Holosphere/Holosphere.ino`, and `core/rotate.h`; `core/FastNoiseLite.h` is treated as a vendored dependency. Letter grades (A+ … F) are measured against a high professional / graphics-middleware bar — not "does it run."*

*This pass post-dates two prior remediation rounds. The findings the prior audits **fixed** were re-checked at the source and confirmed in place (the `hue_rotate` rounding, the `cubic_fast` degenerate fallback, the Hankin `HE_NONE` guard, the IslamicStars topology bound, the `pov_segment_map` mask, the segment-worker teardown, the `buffer_free()` and `Gradient::get` tests). The findings they **dismissed** as false or by-design were not re-raised. New candidates this round were dominated, again, by by-design decisions and dead-code defensive nits that did not survive validation; the few that did are catalogued below.*

## 1. Executive Summary

Holosphere is a single C++20 spherical rendering engine (~50k LOC across `core/`, `effects/`, `hardware/`, `targets/`, plus the sibling `daydream/` JavaScript simulator) that compiles to two Teensy 4.x persistence-of-vision LED-sphere firmware targets — a 96×20 single-controller *Holosphere* and a four-microcontroller 288×144 *Phantasm* — and to a WebAssembly module driving a Three.js browser twin. The same effect classes, the same 335 KB arena, and the same per-frame `Pixel16[]` buffer run unchanged across all three targets; resolution is a compile-time `<W,H>` template parameter, so the device build carries no cost for generality.

The defining property of this codebase, confirmed yet again by sixteen reviewers handed disjoint slices, is **uniformity of judgment**. Independent readers reconstructed the same author values from unrelated files: a fail-fast `HS_CHECK` doctrine placed deliberately on *cold* seams and explicitly kept off the per-pixel hot loop (where a stripped `assert`, documented as a choice, stands in); comments that encode *contracts and rationale* rather than restating code; a 16-bit-linear / OKLCH perceptual color pipeline; and the compile-time variadic `Pipeline<W,H,Filters…>` that multiple reviewers named, unprompted, the strongest abstraction in the tree. The safety story remains *mechanically verified* — a re-exec death harness asserting the exact illegal-instruction status for two dozen cold-path invariants, a determinism harness requiring byte-identical re-renders under a mock clock and pinned RNG, host-testable hardware-protocol and segment-tiling layers, and a WASM zero-copy memory-view lifetime engineered correctly on both sides of the boundary.

What this third pass adds is a sharper picture of *where the remaining debt actually lives*: it is concentrated at the **untrusted and the never-trodden edges**, not in the engine's working core. The two substantive new items are both defensive-boundary gaps. One is genuinely reachable: the WASM `MeshOpsWrapper::fromData()` mesh importer — fed by the `solids.html` editor tool — soft-rejects every other malformed input (bad vertex stride, out-of-range index, oversize mesh) yet silently truncates a face whose vertex count exceeds 255, corrupting topology rather than rejecting it. The other is latent: the `SDF::Face` gnomonic projection divides by `dot(v, basis_v)` with no near-zero guard, where its plot-path siblings have one — unreachable on the manifold solids the product renders, but a real parity gap against the codebase's own pattern. The rest of the surviving list is a comment-clarity nit and two documentation/consistency notes (one of them the still-open param-lerp divergence carried over from the prior audit). No P0 survives; no architectural decision needs reversing.

Equally telling is what was **thrown out**. A claimed missing-orbit-guard turned out to sit in dead code with zero call sites. A claimed truncate-vs-round inconsistency in IslamicStars rested on a false premise — the index it reads is always an exact integer. A claimed constructor "opacity" was not a defect at all. The aggressive reading of the worst candidates again collapsed under a five-minute look at the source.

**Net: A− work, and a tree that has stayed clean across remediation rounds.** A cohesive, philosophically consistent, exceptionally documented and verified engine whose remaining debt is small, mechanical, and sits at the boundary seams rather than the core.

## 3. Cross-Cutting Quality Dimensions

These grades are the whole-product synthesis across all sixteen components.

### Correctness & Robustness — **A−**
After validation, no memory-safety defect (use-after-free, OOB, alignment, arena overflow) was confirmed in the engine core, and the arena bounds math is provably wrap-safe with the proof in the comment. Numerical edges are guarded with uncommon consistency — projections, normalizes, and hot-path divisions carry documented epsilons or `normalized_or` fallbacks, and the NaN/Inf cluster is death-tested to *trap* rather than propagate. The band is set by two confirmed defensive-boundary gaps. The reachable one is the WASM `fromData()` per-face count truncation (`wasm.cpp:560,569`): a tool-driven, untrusted-input path that rejects every *other* malformed shape but silently corrupts a >255-vertex face. The latent one is the unguarded `Face` gnomonic division (`sdf.h:1191-1193`), which cannot fire on the manifold solids the product renders but breaks parity with its own plot-path siblings. Neither mis-paints shipped hardware; one of them can corrupt a mesh in the editor tool. *(Dismissed this pass: a dead-code orbit-guard gap; a truncate-vs-round "inconsistency" resting on a false floating-point-noise premise; a constructor-ordering "opacity" that is not a defect.)*

### Architecture & Design Elegance — **A**
The compile-time `Pipeline<W,H,Filters…>` with automatic world/screen/pixel bridging via discarded `if constexpr` branches remains a genuinely excellent abstraction, and the terminal-filter `static_assert` plus `requires`-clause trait checks make a mis-wired pipeline a *compile* error rather than a silent no-op. The one-engine/three-targets seam leaks nothing into effect code; the owned-vs-borrowed `MeshState` duality (discriminated on `is_bound()`), the purely-functional Conway operators over ping-ponged arenas, the CRTP `ReactionDiffusionBase`, the self-registering `REGISTER_EFFECT` factory with startup roster verification, and the POD `Feedback::Style` are all cohesive. No structural change is indicated anywhere in the tree.

### Interface Expressiveness — **A−**
`registerParam` → WASM → auto-generated GUI is a clean reflection seam; the `Fragment` shader interface with documented per-rasterizer register conventions, `SolidBuilder`'s fluent Conway chaining, `.then()` animation sequencing, and `ArenaSpan`'s type-level borrow-vs-own distinction are all expressive and hard to misuse by accident. Docked a half-step for ergonomic seams a contributor *could* misuse — chiefly the `setClip(y0,y1,x0,x1)` Y-before-X order — and for the two near-twin stereographic shaders (Liquid2D/Flyby) that expose near-identical parameter sets but animate transitions differently with no stated reason.

### Performance & Resource Discipline — **A**
A standout, and clean this pass. Compile-time resolution dispatch, `FunctionRef` zero-allocation type erasure, split-trig LUTs, fast-reject heuristics, the fused three-species graph-Laplacian walk, branchless saturating color math, `fast_cbrt`/`fast_sinf` deployed *exactly* on the OKLab hot path and nowhere else, a single instanced draw call with GPU-side normalize, and worker parallelism measured as `max()` not `sum()`. Memory is rigorous to the byte, with `static_assert`s tying arena budgets to the index types that address them. No new waste was found; the prior per-segment recopy is fixed and verified.

### Documentation & Comments — **A+**
Again the highest-scoring dimension. Comments encode *why*, not *what* — the `lerp16` signed-`smlad` unsoundness, the no-wrap arena-bounds derivation, the relaxed-atomics single-observer proof, the equal-priority-ISR LOAD-BEARING invariant, the `-fno-finite-math-only` ordering, and numerous "do NOT change this" guards that record past bugs. The 1,900-line README is genuine architectural documentation that reviewers used as a contract and found accurate. The only blemish this round is a single undocumented conformal-mapping singularity in MobiusGrid — drift, not error.

### Testing & Verification — **A−**
A verified fail-fast death harness (re-exec, exact illegal-instruction status, 24 invariants), a determinism harness enforcing byte-identical re-renders under an injected clock, an auto-generated smoke pass that constructs and renders every effect at 288×144 with asserts on, hardware wire-format and segment-tiling proofs, and a WASM memory-view stability guard — fronted by three continuous layers (pre-commit hook, presubmit CI, gated deploy). The previously-flagged gaps are now closed and confirmed: the `buffer_free()` spin-wait is exercised by a helper-thread test, and `Gradient::get`'s out-of-range/NaN saturation is asserted. The honest remaining thinness is at the glue and web layers — no fuzzing of the untrusted WASM entry points (exactly where this pass found the live `fromData()` gap), and a single tooling sanity check on the JS side.

### Consistency with Stated Philosophies — **A**
The five design philosophies (16-bit linear color; compile-time resolution; fail-fast cold-only `HS_CHECK`; arena allocation; double-buffer-by-design) are applied with rare uniformity and *enforced at build/startup*, not merely documented. The deductions are the precise spots where the code deviates from its *own* rule: the `Face` gnomonic division that lacks the singularity guard its siblings carry, and the `fromData()` boundary that soft-rejects four malformed shapes but not the fifth. These are local lapses in an otherwise-enforced discipline, which is why they read as fixable nits rather than systemic drift.

## 4. Prioritized Fix List

*Each item independently verified against the source by a separate validator and re-checked here. Severity: P0 = mis-paints real hardware or a latent semantic trap; P1 = robustness / verification / resource risk; P2 = consistency & cosmetic correctness; P3 = doc drift & cleanup.*

**P0 — correctness on real hardware / latent semantic traps**

*None found in scope this pass. Every P0/P1 candidate that survived to validation was either a defensive-boundary gap (listed under P1) or was dismissed on inspection (see §4a). The prior audits' hardware/feedback P0s remain remediated.*

**P1 — robustness / verification / resource risks**

1. ✅ **Fixed.** Reject (don't truncate) a >255-vertex face in `MeshOpsWrapper::fromData()` (`targets/wasm/wasm.cpp:560,569`). The two `m.face_counts.push_back((uint8_t)current_count)` casts silently wrap a face count past 255 — a 256-vertex face becomes a 0-vertex face, corrupting topology — while every *other* malformed-input case in the same function (non-multiple-of-3 vertex stride at `:477`, out-of-range index at `:490`, oversize mesh at `:532`) is soft-rejected with `return nullptr` and a log line. This is the untrusted JS mesh-editor boundary (`solids.html`), so it is reachable. *Resolved by bounding the per-face vertex run to `UINT8_MAX` in the up-front counting pass and soft-rejecting with `return nullptr` before any allocation, mirroring the function's other reject-don't-trap checks; the cast sites are now provably wrap-safe.*

2. Add the antipode guard to `SDF::Face::setup_frame_and_polygon` (`core/sdf.h:1191-1193`). It computes `float d = dot(v, basis_v); px = dot(v, basis_u) / d; py = dot(v, basis_w) / d;` with no near-zero guard, so a mesh vertex projecting to the face center's antipode (`d ≈ 0`) yields ±inf/NaN gnomonic coordinates. The plot-path planar projection guards exactly this (`plot.h:36-40`, `COS_PLANAR_ANTIPODE`), and `fast_atan2` guards its own `0/0` origin (`3dmath.h:252`); `Face` does not. It does not fire on the registered solids (which never produce antipodal in-face vertices), so this is a defensive-parity hardening, not a live bug — clamp `d` away from zero (`if (fabsf(d) < TOLERANCE) d = copysignf(TOLERANCE, d);`) or detect and skip the degenerate vertex with a comment.

**P2 — consistency & cosmetic correctness**

3. Add a one-line comment to the interval re-wrap in `Scan::scan_region` (`core/scan.h:147-149`). `fmodf(iv.first, W)` followed by `if (s < 0) s += W` is correct — `fmodf` of a negative operand is negative in C, and SDF `emit_*` functions can emit a negative start when a shape straddles θ=0 — but the surrounding comment (`:131-138`) explains the *purpose* of wrapping without noting *why a negative can arise* at the point of execution. A single line (`// fmodf(negative, W) is negative; re-wrap into [0,W).`) closes the gap, matching the file's "comments encode why" standard.

**P3 — doc drift & cleanup**

4. Document the conformal-mapping singularity in `MobiusGrid`'s longitude lines (`effects/MobiusGrid.h:174-181`). `R = sqrt((1 + z) / (1 - z))` with `z = sin(t_line · 2π)` is singular at `t_line = 0.25` (`z → 1`), producing inf → `wrap()` → NaN into `palette.get()`. The output is in fact safe — NaN saturates to the palette endpoint via `__builtin_fmaxf` clamp semantics — but the singularity and its intentional, visually-acceptable handling are undocumented, which violates the project's own "NaN-generating conditions are justified by a comment" contract (the prior audit set this precedent documenting the `fast_atan2` epsilon). Add the explanatory comment, or guard with `std::max(1e-6f, 1.0f - z)` if exactness is wanted.

5. Reconcile or document the param-lerp strategy divergence between Liquid2D (`Liquid2D.h:153-183`, staggered per-field time slices) and Flyby (`Flyby.h:119-127`, parallel per-field). Two near-twin stereographic shaders with near-identical parameter sets transition presets with different choreography; Liquid2D documents its staggering, Flyby does not. This is the prior audit's open item #9, still unresolved — either unify the strategy (staggered is the documented one) or add a one-line rationale to Flyby. *(Behavioral-consistency note only; no shared base is proposed, distinct from the previously-rejected StereoShaderBase dedup.)*

### 4a. Verified and dismissed this pass

*Recorded so they are not re-raised in a future audit.*

- **`closest_point_on_mesh_graph` missing `max_orbit` guard (claimed P2)** — *rejected: dead code.* The function (`core/conway.h:1075-1137`) does lack the iteration-count guard that `vertex_orbit` and `relax` carry, but a grep across both repos finds **zero call sites** — it has never shipped. Its input is also guaranteed manifold by `HalfEdgeMesh`'s constructor (which traps on non-manifold build). Hardening unused code is not worth a developer's time; if it is ever wired up, the `relax`/`vertex_orbit` pattern is the obvious fix.
- **IslamicStars truncates `faceIdx` while HankinSolids rounds (claimed P2)** — *rejected: false premise.* The register `v2` is set once to `static_cast<float>(i)` from a `size_t` loop index in `Scan::Mesh::draw` (`scan.h:469`) and never touched by intermediate arithmetic before the shader reads it, so it is *always an exact integer* — truncation and rounding are identical, and there is no floating-point noise to be "robust" against. One `std::round` call site does not establish a pattern. The genuine defensive gap here (the topology bounds check) was a *different* item and is already fixed.
- **`SDF::Face` constructor `edge_vectors` initialization-order opacity (claimed P2)** — *rejected: not a defect.* The data flow (bounds computation populates the scratch arrays → spans are created → helpers read them) is correct and the helpers are force-inlined so codegen matches the original monolith. There is no pattern violation, parity gap, or misleading comment — reordering would be a style preference, below the project's P2 threshold.

*(Carried forward from prior audits and still not defects — do not re-raise: the `normalize()`/`normalized_or()` "epsilon inversion"; the WASM `getVertices()` "use-after-free"; the `BZReactionDiffusion::blend_species` "incorrect formula"; the `FunctionRef::operator()` hot-path `assert`; the StereoShaderBase dedup; the live-param-sync factoring; the reaction-graph `PROGMEM` placement; the Liquid2D `Driver` wrap; the double-buffer "tearing" non-issue; and the `wasm.cpp` §10.7 cross-reference, which is correct as written.)*

## 5. Overall Impression

Holosphere continues to read like the work of one strong engineer who fixed a small set of principles, applied them everywhere, and then *built the machinery to prove they hold* — and, across three audit rounds now, kept them holding. The rare quality is not any single clever file; it is that sixteen reviewers, each handed a different slice with no shared notes, returned the same account of the author's values and the same shape of (now very small) weakness. Most codebases this size are an archaeology of several past selves. This one has a single legible voice, and the voice has stayed consistent through two rounds of remediation.

The more telling result of *this* pass is the geography of what remains. The engine's working core — math, color, memory, the filter pipeline, the animation system, the hardware drivers, two-thirds of the effects — returned **no findings at all**. Everything the audit surfaced lives at an edge: an untrusted JS importer, a projection path the shipped geometry never reaches, a comment, a documentation gap. That distribution is itself a quality signal: defects cluster where a solo author has the least feedback (the boundary fed by a separate tool, the branch no registered input exercises) and vanish where the determinism harness and the death tests give him a tight loop. And the candidates that *didn't* survive — a guard demanded on dead code, an inconsistency premised on floating-point noise that cannot occur — show the validation step doing real work: the aggressive reading of the worst findings dissolved under inspection. None of the residue is rot. All of it is the ordinary debt of velocity, concentrated exactly where a second pair of hands would have been watching.

## 6. Technical & Artistic Merit — The Achievement

The achievement is best understood as **three hard problems solved at once on one body of code, held at bit parity**:

1. **A real-time spherical rendering engine** with its own SDF scanline rasterizer, geodesic curve plotter, volumetric ray-marcher, a half-edge mesh kernel carrying the full Conway operator algebra, a perceptual (OKLCH) 16-bit-linear color pipeline, and a compile-time-composed filter graph — all templated to zero overhead at a fixed resolution.

2. **Hard-real-time POV firmware** that paints a sphere by spinning an LED strip at 480 RPM, firing columns on a microsecond ISR budget, double-buffered lock-free — and, in the Phantasm build, *coordinated across four microcontrollers* over two sync wires with a branchless per-segment ISR and a deterministic cross-board RNG, so all four paint an identical canvas with zero inter-board phase drift.

3. **A browser digital twin** that compiles the identical C++ to WebAssembly, renders it through Three.js with zero-copy GPU-normalized pixel readback, and reproduces the four-board partitioning in generation-fenced Web Workers — so the hardware layout can be exercised, and now *fuzzed*, in software before fabrication.

The artistic layer is mathematically literate rather than decorative. The effects implement the *actual* Hopf fibration of S³ with a 4D tumble, the associated-Legendre recurrence for real spherical harmonics, Belousov–Zhabotinsky and Gray–Scott reaction–diffusion on a precomputed 7,680-node Fibonacci K-NN graph (with a fused three-species Laplacian walk), authentic Hankin-method Islamic star patterns generated procedurally from Conway-operated Archimedean solids, twisted-torus volumetrics with metallic Blinn–Phong and an analytic Lipschitz bound for safe sphere-tracing, and Möbius-warped grids with pole-stabilizing counter-rotation. These are written by someone who understands the geometry, not assembled from shader-toy fragments — and each is correctly guarded at its singularities, which is the part that is genuinely hard to get right.

## 7. Comparative Appraisal — Independent Assessment

*Formed from first principles. Where the prior audit ranked Holosphere against four reference populations in turn, this assessment asks a different question of each peer class: not "is it better or worse," but **"what does each side have that the other structurally cannot?"** — because the interesting fact about this artifact is the trade it represents.*

**What kind of object is this?** A *fabrication-grade renderer for one exotic display geometry*, instantiated simultaneously as a microcontroller firmware, a four-node distributed real-time system, and a WebAssembly module, with a software twin maintained at bit parity. Almost nothing else occupies all four roles, so the honest comparison is per-axis rather than overall.

**Versus the maker / addressable-LED world (FastLED, WLED, one-off POV rigs).** What that ecosystem has that Holosphere does not: breadth of hardware support, a community of contributors, and an enormous library of ready effects across thousands of board configurations. What Holosphere has that the ecosystem structurally cannot produce: a perceptual linear-light pipeline, a zero-overhead compile-time-specialized engine, a *verified* fail-fast safety net, a CI-gated deploy, a byte-faithful simulator, and synchronized multi-controller POV — none of which a single-target Arduino sketch with 8-bit sRGB blending and tuned magic constants is built to express. The two are not points on one scale; they optimize different things, and on the *engineering* axis the gap is categorical.

**Versus professional creative-technology tooling (Pixelblaze at the embedded end; MadMapper / TouchDesigner / architectural-LED controllers at the workstation end).** What the commercial products have: generality (they map *any* geometry, not just a sphere), polish, support, and the test-and-calendar depth a team buys — fuzzing, soak testing, mechanical enforcement of invariants. What Holosphere matches them on, inside its narrow scope: the *practices* a serious graphics team would recognize — compile-time specialization, an explicit documented memory model, host-testable protocol layers, build-provenance hashing, a reflection-driven GUI, and a determinism harness. The honest read is that the core is product-quality and the *seams* are single-authored: this pass found its live defect precisely in an untrusted glue path with no fuzzing, which is exactly the dimension a hardened product spends headcount on.

**Versus academic / research code.** What research artifacts typically have that this lacks: a *novel* primitive — a new algorithm, a new result. Holosphere is **integrative, not novel**: OKLab, SDF CSG, sphere tracing, the Hopf fibration, and Conway operators are all established art. What it has that the median research prototype structurally lacks: *reproducibility and survivability.* The usual research failure mode — a brilliant one-off that no one can rebuild or trust — is inverted here; the determinism harness and the bit-faithful twin make every claimed frame replayable, and the death tests prove the safety net fires. As a research artifact its standout virtue is methodological, not algorithmic.

**Versus the state of the art.** It does not move the rendering or color-science frontier and does not try to. Where it plausibly *is* at the frontier is the specific conjunction it occupies — a perceptually-correct, compile-time-specialized, formally-fail-fast renderer that runs bit-identically across a four-microcontroller POV sphere and a WebAssembly browser twin, with a death harness proving its invariants trap. It would be genuinely surprising to find a more rigorous *open* instance of that exact intersection; the frontier it touches is one of integration and verification, not of any single primitive.

**Standing.** As solo work this is exceptional, and three audit rounds — the latter two spent mostly *retiring* candidate findings rather than confirming them — raise rather than lower that read. The grade's ceiling is set not by anything present but by what a team-and-calendar practice would add: fuzzing of the untrusted WASM entry points (where this pass found its one reachable defect), mechanical enforcement of the consistency invariants the author currently maintains by discipline, and the second reviewer who turns "guard present in the plot path, missing in the SDF path" into "guard present in both." That gap — superb individual work versus battle-hardened collective work — is the entire distance between this **A−** and an A. It is a deficit of headcount, not of craft or vision.
